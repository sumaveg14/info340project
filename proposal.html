Problem & Data Domain

Minecraft is a sandbox video game where players explore, build, and survive in generated worlds made up of many different biomes. Each biome has unique environmental characteristics, structures, mobs and resources that directly affect gameplay. As the game has continued to grow over time, the number of biomes and materials has grown significantly, making it much more difficult for players to keep track of where specific resources can be found or what each biome contains. In order to fully enjoy the capabilities of the game, its important to educate the enthusiasts!

The primary information problem in this space is information overload. Existing resources such as the Minecraft Wiki and community made fandom pages give many details, but they are often text-heavy, and difficult to go through while actively playing the game. Players frequently need quick, information like which biomes contain certain materials or structures, but current tools are not designed for fast filtering, personalization, or interactive exploration. Video tutorials and guides on platforms like YouTube can also be helpful, but they are time-consuming and not easily searchable for specific needs. Because of this, both new and experienced players struggle to efficiently access biome-related information in a way that supports real-time decision-making during gameplay.
